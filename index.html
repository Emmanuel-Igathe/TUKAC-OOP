📜 About TUK Ability Club

History

Founded in 2018 by a group of visionary students and faculty, TUKAC was established to champion accessibility, equal opportunity, and social integration for PwDs. Over the years, the club has grown into a vibrant community hosting awareness campaigns, assistive tech workshops, and peer mentorship programs.

Constitution

Article 1: Name – Technical University of Kenya Ability Club (TUKAC).
Article 2: Objectives – Promote inclusion, advocate for accessible infrastructure, organize capacity-building events, and foster leadership among PwDs.
Article 3: Membership – Open to all TUK students & staff; members pay semester contribution fees (KES 500).
Article 4: Governance – Executive committee elected annually; advisors support operations.
Article 5: Amendments – Changes require 2/3 majority of active members.
Full constitution available upon request.
